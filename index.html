Unique Kakanin of the Philippines

Kakanin are traditional Filipino pastries made with glutinous rice, coconut milk, and sugar. They are prepared in different ways across regions, making them a symbol of local tradition and culture.

Inutak (Taguig, Metro Manila)

A gooey baked kakanin made of galapong and coconut milk, topped with ube.

Tupig (Ilocos & Pangasinan)

A sweet grilled rice cake wrapped in banana leaves, filled with coconut and sugar.

Daral (Sulu)

A Tausug delicacy made of thin coconut-milk crepes filled with sweetened coconut strips, rolled like a spring roll.

Panyalam (Mindanao)

A crispy deep-fried kakanin made from ground rice, sugar, and coconut milk.

Binaki (Bukidnon, Mindanao)

A sweet steamed corn cake wrapped in corn husks, similar to tamales but uniquely Filipino.
