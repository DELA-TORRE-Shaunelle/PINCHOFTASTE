Local Pastries Recipes

Inutak (Taguig, Metro Manila)

A gooey baked kakanin made of galapong and coconut milk, topped with ube.

Ingredients:
  • 2 cups glutinous rice flour
  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup ube halaya
  • 1/4 cup butter
Procedure:
  1. Mix ingredients until smooth.
  2. Pour mixture into a greased baking dish.
  3. Bake at 180°C until partly set.
  4. Add ube and butter, continue baking.
  5. Serve warm.
Tupig (Ilocos & Pangasinan)

A grilled rice cake wrapped in banana leaves.

Ingredients:
  • 2 cups glutinous rice flour
  • 1 cup grated coconut
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • Banana Leaves
Procedure:
  1. Mix dry and wet ingredients.
  2. Wrap in banana leaves.
  3. Grill until cooked.
Daral (Sulu)

Thin coconut-milk crepes filled with sweetened coconut strips.

Ingredients:
  • Flour
  • Coconut Milk
  • Eggs
  • Grated Coconut
  • Sugar
Procedure:
  1. Make crepes.
  2. Cook coconut filling.
  3. Roll and serve.
Panyalam (Mindanao)

A crispy deep-fried kakanin.

Ingredients:
  • Rice Flour
  • Coconut Milk
  • Sugar
Procedure:
  1. Mix batter.
  2. Deep fry until golden.
Binaki (Bukidnon, Mindanao)

A steamed corn cake wrapped in corn husks.

Ingredients:
  • Grated Corn
  • Cornmeal
  • Coconut Milk
  • Sugar
Procedure:
  1. Mix ingredients.
  2. Wrap in corn husks.
  3. Steam until firm.
These kakanin show the rich diversity of Filipino pastries — each bite tells a story of tradition and culture.
